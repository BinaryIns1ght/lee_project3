Welcome To Edutainment To A Higher Degree

This is a festival to help push forth education to many people. Many children don't have a chance getting their education, having them be left behind in understanding the world around us. It is up to this festival to help fund for many low-income students to be able to attend the community colleges around the U.S.

This festival features many events to really push your intelligence, while priotizing your entertainment. The festival was built off of an old amusement park, so many of the old attractions can be provided and refurbished for your welcoming fun.
Do keep in mind to be patient if a long line is being built to re-experience these rides again.

The festival also has small marketplaces by the field so you can purchase various goods provided by the events. From simple clothing, to the most delectable foods, you can confirm to get the best products here.

While you enjoy the festives, you can explore the park to see some of nature's real beauties. View the natural world in its rawest state. Within the park, you can see many of nature's breathtaking structures, from waterfalls to the fields of flowers. If wildlife is spotted, best to inform the staff on any issue this would bring.

This park is also incredibly eco-friendly. Many festivals/carnivals usually use so much natural resources it seems quite detrimental to the natural world. We, however, renovated the park to push forth a more environmentally-focused design philosophy around it, so no worries if the park would cause any issues.

The sponsor for the festival is "One Million Degrees". This is an Illinois-based charity with a focus with aiding low-income students get into their desired community college. All proceeds from the festival, from the tickets to the products from the events.

Founder Of the Festival
Hello, my name is Johnathan Hitchseed. I am a professor from Illinois State University. I often overlooked how education is very important, but as of lately, I've seen many students never looked into many information that you can commonly see in books. I thought to make this festival to help push education to a larger audience.
